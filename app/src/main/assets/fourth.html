Timeline of History in Virginia

1000-1400: Monacan Indian remains from west-central Virginia.

1570: Spanish Jesuits establish the Ajacan mission on the York River. All 8 murdered the following year by Indians.

1607: Captain Christopher Newport and 105 followers found Jamestown.

1624: James I revokes Virginia's charter after years of unprofitable operations and it became a royal colony.

1676: Bacon's Rebellion against English Governor occurs and the city of Jamestown is burned.

1698: Williamsburg becomes the capital of Virginia.

1781: The British are defeated at the Battle of Yorktown and the fighting in the Revolutionary War comes to an end.

1783: Virginia emancipates slaves who fought for independence during the Revolutionary War.

1788: Virginia becomes the 10th state of the USA.

1805: The state of Virginia passes a law requiring all freed slaves to leave the state, or risk either imprisonment or deportation.

1814: British forces land on the Patuxent River and route the Americans in the Battle of Bladensburg, and then march to Washington.

1859: Abolitionist John Brown leads five black and 13 white supporters in a raid on the federal armory at Harper's Ferry hoping to arm the slaves in a revolt.

1861: Virginia is the eighth state to secede from the Union and joins the Confederate States and the Civil War begins.

1865: Robert E. Lee surrenders to the Union Army at Appomattox signaling the end of the Civil War.

1870: Virginia is readmitted to the Union.

1908: Orville Wright makes the 1st 1-hr airplane flight at Fort Myer, VA.

1927: The Bristol Sessions, a series of recording sessions, take place in in Bristol, and help spread "hillbilly music" (AKA "Country") to the rest of the country.

1960: The USS Enterprise, the first nuclear-powered aircraft carrier, is launched at Newport News, VA.

1981: Elizabeth Jordan Carr, the first American test-tube baby, is born in Norfolk, VA.

1994: In Manassas, Va., Lorena Bobbitt goes on trial, charged with malicious wounding of her husband, John. She had cut off her husband's male organ, and is acquitted by reason of temporary insanity.

2001: The Pentagon is crashed into by a hijacked passenger jet as part of the 9-11 terrorist attacks. Ground breaking ceremonies were held exactly 60 years ago.
